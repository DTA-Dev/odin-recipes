Fried Chicken

Fried chicken is a beloved dish known for its crispy, golden-brown crust and tender, juicy meat. The key to a great fried chicken recipes lies in seasoning, marinating, and the frying technique. Start by choosing bone-in, skin-on chicken pieces like thighs, drumsticks or breasts, as the bones and skin help keep the meat moist and flavourful. A buttermilk marinade is a classic choice because its acidity helps tenderise the chicken while adding tanginess. Season the marinade generously with salt, pepper, garlic powder, paprika, and any other favourite spices.

For the coating, mix flour with a blend of seasonings such a cayenne, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper for extra flavour. After marinating, dredge each piece of chicken in the seasoned flour, pressing it firmly to create a thick, even coating. To ensure extra crispiness, some people double dip the chicken by first dreding it in flour, then dipping it in a buttermilk or an egg wash, and finally coating it with flour again. Fry the chicken in hot oil (around 175C) until it reaches an internal temperature of 75C, turning occasionally for an even golden crust. Let the fried chicken rest on a wire rack to drain excess oil, ensuring it stays crispy. The result is succulent, flavourful chicken with a perfectly crunchy exterior.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 5 cups vegetable oil for frying

Steps to Cook:

  1. Whisk together buttermilk, mustard, salt, pepper, and cayenne in a bowl.
  2. Pour buttermilk marinade into a resealable plastic bag. Add chicken pieces, coat with marinade, squeeze out excess air, and seal the bag. Marinate in the refrigerator for 2 to 8 hours.
  3. Combine flour, baking powder, garlic powder, and onion powder in another resealable plastic bag; shake to mix thoroughly.
  4. Working with one piece at a time, transfer marinated chicken to the dry ingredient bag, seal the bag, and shake well to coat. After all chicken pieces are coated, repeat the process by dipping them in buttermilk marinade and shaking in the dry coating again. Then heat oil in a large frying pan over medium-high heat. Place chicken on the pan.
  5. Fry chicken in batches in hot oil, turning chicken occasionally, until golden brown and juices run clear. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the meat should read at least 165 degrees F (74 degrees C).
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!
